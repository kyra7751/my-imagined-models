## 1. Introduction

General relativity (Einstein, 1915) successfully explains gravity from the solar system to cosmology, but suffers from singularities at black hole centres and the Big Bang. Quantum mechanics introduces the Planck scale $L_p = \sqrt{\hbar G/c^3}$ as a fundamental limit, yet its integration into gravity remains incomplete.

Vortex Theory arises from the intuition that the universe is built from simple geometric patterns: inward pressure ($x^{-2}$) and balancing vibration ($x^{2}$). By combining $1/r^2$ (Vortex In) and $r^2$ (Vortex Out), the theory eliminates singularities and provides measurable corrections to general relativity.

## 2. Theoretical Derivation

### 2.1 Core Vortex Formula

Mass $m$ creates spacetime pressure proportional to $c^4$ and bounded by the Planck length $L_p$:

$$
\boxed{V_x = \frac{m c^4 L_p}{r^2}} \qquad [\text{Vx}]
$$

The unit $V_x$ ($\text{M L}^3 \text{T}^{-4}$) is the rate of spatial pressure. The $1/r^2$ form recalls the inverse‑square law, and $L_p$ ensures finiteness as $r \to 0$.

### 2.2 Duality Balance Formula

Inward pressure (Vortex In) must be balanced by outward response (Vortex Out) of the form $r^2$. Symmetry under $r \leftrightarrow L_p^2/r$ gives:

$$
\boxed{V_{\text{total}} = m c^2 \left( \frac{L_p^2}{r^2} + \frac{r^2}{L_p^2} \right)}
$$

Minimum at $r = L_p$ with $V_{\min}=2mc^2$. The force is:

$$
F = -\frac{dV}{dr} = 2 m c^2 \left( \frac{L_p^2}{r^3} - \frac{r}{L_p^2} \right)
$$

At $r = L_p$, $F=0$. Small oscillations give frequency $\omega = 2c/L_p$ (Planck harmonic oscillator).

### 2.3 Complete Lagrangian

The Lagrangian combines Newtonian mechanics, post-Newtonian corrections, Planck-scale vortex effects, and the cosmological constant $\Lambda$:

$$
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal{L} = &\ \frac{1}{2}m(\dot{r}^2 + r^2\dot{\theta}^2) + \frac{GMm}{r} \\
&+ \frac{3}{2}\frac{GMm}{c^2 r}(\dot{r}^2 + r^2\dot{\theta}^2) + \frac{m}{8c^2}(\dot{r}^2 + r^2\dot{\theta}^2)^2 - \frac{G^2 M^2 m}{2c^2 r^2} \\
&- m c^2 \left[ \frac{L_p^2}{r^2} e^{-r/L_p} - \frac{\Lambda}{6} r^2 \right]
\end{aligned}
}
$$

- Terms 1–2: Newtonian kinetic + potential.
- Terms 3–5: first‑order post‑Newtonian corrections (tested by Mercury’s perihelion precession).
- Term 6: Planck‑scale vortex (exponential cutoff, removes singularity).
- Term 7: cosmological constant (dark energy).

### 2.4 Vortex Metric (Regular Black Hole)

Modifying the Schwarzschild metric with a vortex term yields:

$$
\boxed{f(r) = 1 - \frac{2GM}{c^2 r} + \frac{\gamma L_p^2}{r^2}}, \qquad ds^2 = -f(r) c^2 dt^2 + \frac{dr^2}{f(r)} + r^2 d\Omega^2
$$

- $\gamma \sim 1$ (dimensionless).
- As $r \to 0$, $f(r) \sim \gamma L_p^2/r^2 > 0$ → no singularity.
- Horizons from $f(r)=0$ give two roots, modified at Planck scale.
